<p>One of the most versatile constraint types in Rigid Body Dynamics (RBD) simulations is the Cone Twist Constraint. You can make it behave like a pin constraint or a glue constraint by placing limitations on its degrees of freedom, and it can also behave like anything in between. Cone Twist Constraints can add that next level of realism to dynamic relationships, yet it's not as widely used as it could be or setup incorrectly due partly to a lack of documentation and perhaps understanding of how it works.</p>

<p>So here are a few examples to hopefully clarify how the system behaves.</p>

{{< /postText >}} <!--postText-->

 <h2>The Basics</h2>

<p>Like the name suggests, the Cone Twist Constraint comprises of two components, the cone component which is constrained by <mark>constrained_twist_axis</mark> (source of confusing through unfortunate naming), and the twisting component which is constrained by <mark>constrained_up_axis</mark>. Below we illustrate what each component does separately.</p>

 <h3>Twist Component</h3>
<p>Using the same model, the twist component allows for rotational movement around the constrained_twist_axis, where the angle of rotation is limited through the <mark>constrained_up_axis</mark>.</p>
